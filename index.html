Spatiotemporal Modeling Using INLA
with R

This project applied the Integrated Nested Laplace Approximation (INLA) method to analyze and predict the spatiotemporal distribution of sei whales in the Azores. Environmental covariates such as sea surface temperature- and water depth were examined to evaluate their influence on whale occurrence. The model incorporated different detection functions to account for factors affecting detection probability, including survey effort, distance to observer vessel, and visibility conditions. The work involved web scraping, data cleaning, spatiotemporal modeling, model diagnostics and comparison, as well as visualizing hotspots of whale activity.

A/B Test
with MySQL

Hate this. I still don’t really understand why everyone emphasizes A/B testing so much. When I first faced it in interviews, I didn’t even know what it was. Later, I looked it up and realized it’s basically just hypothesis testing applied in a business context—something we learned in the very first statistics class. I hadn’t used it for a long time, so I had forgotten, and I never imagined it would become so crucial for getting a job. I actually loved statistics, but the job-hunting process has made me resent it. Why do I have to prove that I can do A/B tests? I’m pretty sure ChatGPT could do it—and better than I could.
